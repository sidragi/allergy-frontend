Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Symptoms: Dry, itchy patches, redness, scaly skin

Common Triggers: Stress, irritants, allergens

Body Areas: Hands, elbows, knees, face

Contact Dermatitis

Symptoms: Red rash, blisters, burning sensation

Common Triggers: Nickel, latex, cosmetics, poison ivy

Body Areas: Where skin touched allergen

Hives (Urticaria)

Symptoms: Raised welts, itching, swelling

Common Triggers: Foods, medications, insect stings

Body Areas: Anywhere on body

Skin Allergy Comparison

Key differences between common skin allergy conditions.

Feature Eczema Contact Dermatitis Hives
Appearance Dry, scaly patches Red rash with clear borders Raised, red welts
Duration Chronic (weeks-months) 2-4 weeks after exposure Hours to days
Itch Level Intense Moderate to severe Mild to intense
Common Treatments Moisturizers, topical steroids Avoid triggers, antihistamines Antihistamines, cool compresses
